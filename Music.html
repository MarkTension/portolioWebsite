Music

This music builds on earlier work by Japanese artists united under the earth/environmental music genre. Environmental music is mostly inspired by nature. In 80's Japan it gained a lot of traction during the vast urbanisation of tokyo, and in my opinion it's the most interesting time/place within the scene.

My own music project, Spring Walk Collection, with the persona of Saida Kizuki is a tribute to this time and place. It's an experiment with the jazz-like complexity of nature soundscapes and unpredictability of rain. The resulting songs can reside in ambience, but carries the melodic structure and complex rhythms that speak emotionally as well. My project uses samples from native instruments, and applies machine learning methods for melody and rhythm generation.
